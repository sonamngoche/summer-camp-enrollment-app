I gave many prompts to the LLM during this project, too many to fully retrieve or paste into one document. However, I can say that most of my prompts revolved around troubleshooting. Following the concrete instructions of creating my app was simple enough (especially with AI assistance) but most of the trouble I had was with connecting my PostgreSQL database and getting the tables to show up. The LLM was very helpful for code generation and troubleshooting, but definitely had some limitations with the latter. It was more helpful with internal code analysis rather than external assistance or tool navigation. Those were aspects I had to manually adjust. Aside from that, many of my prompts were trying to navigate Retool (which was a struggle with AI).

My system tracks summer camp enrollment and registration. I chose this topic because I worked at a summer camp for several years and thought it would be applicable and fun; especially because our tracking system was on Excel and had to be manually updated. As I have gotten further into MIS, that is something I've thought a lot about in terms of system optimization and creating efficiencies. The hardest part of the project was actually at the end, after all the coding was done. Getting output to display despite successful running of code blocks was incredibly frustrating. Microsoft Copilot was very helpful, especially when I deviated and attempted a different solution. There wasn't really much I changed mid-project that I didn't plan for; I had a clear image of what I wanted to include in my system and it all made the cut. If I had another week, I would add an email function. All of the relevant contact information is in the system, so it would be simple (in theory) to generate an automatic confirmation email after enrolling. It would also confirm if the system contains all relevant information/works properly.
